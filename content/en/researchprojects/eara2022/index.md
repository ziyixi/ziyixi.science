I have developed a new three-dimensional Earth's wave speed model, East Asia Radially Anisotropic Model 2022 (EARA2022), revealing detailed seismic structures of the crust and upper mantle beneath Eurasia and the Western Pacific subduction zone on full waveform inversion (FWI). I construct model EARA2022 by iteratively minimizing the waveform similarity misfit between the synthetics and observed waveforms from 142 earthquakes recorded on about 2,000 broadband stations in Eurasia, mainly in East Asia.
{{< figure src="images/researchprojects/eara2022/eara2022-data.png" >}}

Compared to the previous studies, this new generation of FWI model, after 20 iterations, renders significantly improved images of the large-scale subducted oceanic plate morphology in the upper mantle, the transition zone, and the uppermost of the lower mantle along the Kuril, Japan, Izu-Bonin, and Ryukyu trenches, implied by high wave speed anomalies reaching a maximum of 8% for $V_p$ and 13% for $V_s$. The low wave speed anomalies in the depth range of the asthenosphere, especially in the mantle wedges and backarc basins, suggest the existence of melt due to either high-temperature anomalies or the addition of volatiles, which are depicted by a maximum of 6% wave speed reduction for both $V_p$ and $V_s$.

Moreover, the new model provides new insight into the origins of the intraplate volcanoes in East Asia. For example, one of my findings is the "hole" beneath the Changbaishan volcano, which could bring new insight into why there is a volcano over there.
